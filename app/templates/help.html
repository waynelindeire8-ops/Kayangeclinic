User Manual

Kayange Clinic Management System
Contents
1. Getting Started
System Access

Open your web browser and navigate to your clinic's system URL. You will see the login page.

Default Login: Username: admin | Password: admin123
Change your password immediately after first login via Settings > Profile.
Login Steps
1Enter your username
2Enter your password
3Click Login
4You will be redirected to the Dashboard
Navigation

The sidebar menu on the left gives you access to all modules. The menu items shown depend on your user role. Click the toggle button (top-left) to collapse/expand the sidebar.

  • Dashboard — Overview of key metrics
  • Patients — Patient records and document management
  • Appointments — Scheduling and walk-in queue
  • Additional modules as per your role
2. Patient Management
Adding a New Patient
1Go to Patients in the sidebar
2Click New Patient
3Fill in required fields (First Name, Last Name are mandatory)
4Optional: DOB (DD/MM/YYYY or YYYY-MM-DD), Gender, Phone, Email
5Select Insurance Scheme from dropdown
6Add allergies, medical history, and current medications as needed
7Click Save

The system auto-generates a Patient ID (e.g., KMC-1001).

Viewing & Editing Patients

The patients list shows all registered patients. Use the search bar to filter by name, ID, or phone. Click the icon to view full details, or to edit.

Bulk Import from Excel
1Go to Patients > Import
2Click Choose File and select your .xlsx file
3Click Import Patients

Your Excel must have at least First Name and Last Name columns. Other supported column names include: DOB, Gender, Phone, Email, Address, Insurance, Scheme Type, Policy Number, Blood Group, Emergency Contact, Emergency Phone.

Available insurance providers shown on the import page. Use exact names to auto-link scheme.
Uploading Scanned Documents
1Open a patient's detail page
2Click the Documents tab
3Select a file (PDF, JPEG, PNG, GIF, WEBP — max 50MB)
4Add optional notes (e.g., "Old physical file page 1-5")
5Click Upload

Documents can be viewed/downloaded or deleted from the patient's Documents tab.

3. Appointments & Scheduling
Creating an Appointment
1Go to Appointments > New Appointment
2Select the patient (start typing name to search)
3Choose appointment type: Phone, Online, or Walk-in
4Select date, time, and doctor
5Add reason for visit (optional)
6Click Save
Calendar View

The calendar view shows all appointments. You can switch between day, week, and month views. Click any appointment to view or edit details.

Walk-in Queue

From the dashboard, use the queue panel to register walk-in patients. Their status automatically updates as they move through waiting, in-progress, and completed states.

4. Clinical Consultations
Starting a Consultation
1Go to Consultations
2Click New Consultation
3Select the patient
4Choose consultation type and link to appointment (optional)
Recording Vital Signs

In the consultation form, enter: Blood Pressure (systolic/diastolic), Heart Rate, Temperature, O2 Saturation, Weight, Height. BMI is calculated automatically.

Examinations

Record physical exam findings by body system: General, Cardiovascular, CNS, Respiratory, Abdomen, Musculoskeletal, Skin, and Others.

Diagnoses

Add primary, secondary, or complication diagnoses. Each can include an ICD code and notes.

Prescribing Medications

Within a consultation, click the Prescriptions section. Select drugs from the pharmacy inventory, set dosage, frequency, and duration. The system checks insurance authorization rules automatically.

Ancillary Services

From the consultation, you can order: Lab Tests, Procedures, Medical Certificates, Referrals (auto-adds MWK 20,000 call-out fee), or Diet Support. Each is recorded and linked to the patient's record.

5. Laboratory
Ordering Lab Tests
1Go to Lab > New Order
2Select the patient
3Choose tests from the catalog (80+ tests in 12 categories)
4Click Save
Entering Results

Lab staff can enter results for each parameter. Reference ranges are shown, and the system auto-flags results as NORMAL, HIGH, or LOW based on the patient's gender and age (where configured).

Lab Invoicing

Lab tests can be billed separately via the Lab Invoices module. Invoices use LAB-XXXX numbering format.

The lab catalog includes categories: Haematology, Parasitology, Microbiology, Chemistry, Lipogram, U&E, Cardiac, Serology, Tumour Markers, Endocrinology, Fertility & Steroids, and Radiology.
6. Radiology / Imaging
Ordering Radiology Exams
1Go to Radiology > New Order
2Select patient and modality (X-ray, Ultrasound, CT, MRI, ECG, Echo, Mammogram, Fluoroscopy, Other)
3Set priority: Routine, Urgent, or STAT
4Enter clinical history and indication
5Click Save
External Referrals

If the clinic cannot perform the exam, select "Refer Out". The system will automatically add the MWK 20,000 call-out fee to the patient's billing.

7. Pharmacy
Managing Inventory

Go to Pharmacy > Inventory to view, add, edit, or adjust stock. Low-stock items are highlighted. You can filter to show only low-stock items.

Dispensing Drugs
1Go to Pharmacy > Dispensing
2Select the patient — their insurance scheme auto-loads
3Select a drug — stock level and scheme price auto-display
4Enter quantity — total auto-calculated
5Click Dispense — stock deducted automatically
Insurance Scheme Pricing

You can set different prices per drug per insurance provider. Go to Inventory, click the icon on any drug to configure scheme prices. At dispensing, the correct price is loaded based on the patient's scheme.

8. Billing & Invoicing
Creating an Invoice
1Go to Billing > New Invoice
2Select the patient
3Add line items: select service type (Consultation, Lab, Procedure, Pharmacy, Certificate, Package)
4Enter amounts and click Add Item
5Record payment (full or partial)
6Click Save

Invoice numbers are auto-generated (INV-XXXX). Unbilled services from consultations can be found using the "Unbilled" feature.

Service Packages

The system supports pre-defined packages: Annual Checkup, Maternity, Diabetes Management, and Executive Wellness. Select a package to auto-populate its services.

9. Insurance Module
Managing Providers

Go to Insurance > Providers to view, add, edit, or deactivate insurance companies. 17+ providers are pre-seeded.

Patient Policies

Assign policies to patients with scheme type, policy number, start/end dates. The system checks eligibility status (Active, Expired, Not Yet Effective).

Claims Processing

Full claims lifecycle with 10 statuses:

Draft → Submitted → In Review → Additional Info Requested → Approved / Partially Approved / Denied → Appealed → Paid / Cancelled

Add claim items with amounts, supporting documents, and track status changes with full audit history.

Authorization Rules

Configure rules that require pre-authorization for specific items (e.g., MedHealth requires prior auth for drips/IV fluids). The system alerts you during prescribing if authorization is needed.

10. Telemedicine
Starting a Session
1Go to Telemedicine > New Session
2Select patient, doctor, session type (Video/Audio/Chat)
3Set scheduled date and time
4Click Save
Conducting a Session

Patients and doctors receive secure tokens to join the session. Use the waiting room before starting. During the session, you can chat in real-time, issue e-prescriptions, and process payments.

11. Short-Stay Unit
Admitting a Patient
1Go to Short Stay
2Click New Admission
3Select patient and resource type (Bed or Drip Station)
4Select an available resource (status shown)
5Click Admit

On discharge, the bed/drip station is automatically marked as Available (or Cleaning if needed).

12. Medical Certificates

Go to Certificates to view statistics and manage certificates. Six types available:

  • Sick Note — Medical leave certification
  • TEP — Travel/Exit Permit
  • Insurance — Insurance medical report
  • Police — Police medical examination form
  • Yellow Book — International vaccination certificate
  • Foreign Stamp — Foreign medical stamp
13. Messages & Notifications
Internal Messaging

Communicate with other staff members. Go to Messages to view Inbox, Sent messages, or compose a new message. Messages support read receipts and soft-deletion.

Notifications

The bell icon in the top bar shows unread notifications. The system sends notifications automatically for:

  • New lab/radiology orders
  • Appointment assignments
  • Message received
  • Low stock alerts
  • Claim status changes
14. Reports & Analytics

The Reports page provides analytics including:

  • Patient Demographics — Gender distribution, insurance coverage stats
  • Revenue Reports — Filterable by date range, with totals and charts
  • Appointment Statistics — By status and type
  • Inventory Report — Total stock value, expiring items
  • Consultation Stats — Consultation counts, types, doctor workload
  • Procedure Stats — Procedure frequency and types
  • Audit Logs — All user actions with timestamps and IP addresses

The Dashboard shows a real-time snapshot: total patients, today's appointments, today's revenue, low-stock items, staff counts, and a 7-day revenue trend chart.

15. Administration
User Management

Admin users can manage all system users: create, edit, delete, toggle active/inactive, and reset passwords. Roles available:

Admin, Doctor, Locum Doctor, Nurse, Locum Nurse, Lab Supervisor, Lab Staff, Lab Tech, Lab Care, Front Desk, File Manager

System Settings

Go to Settings > System to configure:

  • Clinic name, address, phone, email
  • Timezone (default: Africa/Blantyre)
  • Date format (default: DD/MM/YYYY)
  • Currency (default: MWK)
  • Low stock threshold
  • Appointment slot duration
Cloud Backup

Visit /backup (admin-only) to sync your local database to Supabase PostgreSQL cloud. You can:

  • Sync all tables at once
  • Sync individual tables
  • Restore from cloud to local
  • Initialize/update Supabase table schemas
Perform backups regularly to prevent data loss.
16. Troubleshooting
Q: I can't log in.
Ensure your caps lock is off. Passwords are case-sensitive. Contact your admin to reset your password.
Q: Patient not appearing in list after adding.
Click the refresh icon or reload the page (Ctrl+Shift+R). Check your search filter — it may be filtering results.
Q: Excel import failed.
Ensure your file is .xlsx format (not .csv renamed). Check that "First Name" and "Last Name" columns exist. DOB must be in YYYY-MM-DD or DD/MM/YYYY format.
Q: Document upload stuck.
File must be under 50MB. Allowed types: PDF, JPEG, PNG, GIF, WEBP. Check your internet connection.
Q: Page looks broken or buttons not working.
Try a hard refresh (Ctrl+Shift+R). If the issue persists, clear your browser cache and reload.
Q: System is slow.
Try refreshing the page. Check your internet connection. If on Vercel, the first request after inactivity may be slow (cold start).
